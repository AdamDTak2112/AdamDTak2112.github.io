# About Adam

I originally hail from Idaho Falls, Idaho where I grew up through most of my life. I currently reside in Logan, Utah where I recently finished my Bachelors of Science in Philosophy, with a minor in Computer Science at Utah State University.

## Why Computer Science?

Ever since I was a child, I loved technology. We had a computer in the basement that I would spend many-a-night playing old DOS games on, like Privateer, Oregon Trail, and     Where in the World is Carmen San Diego. When I moved to Logan to start at Utah State, I fell in love with programming as a result of this already profound love of tech. Being able to solve real-world problems in such an abstract and modern way excites me endlessly, and I have truly found my calling through this passion!


## More importantly... Why Philosophy?!

I have answered this question more times than I can count, but I always love pointing out the similarities between these two fields of study. In software development, we are constantly trying to solve problems by developing algorithms. This is what we can call "symbolized logic". In philosophy, there exists a school of thought that this symbology is how logic ought to be approached in philosophy - they call themselves the "analytical" school of philosophy. As such, philososphy has helped me endlessly in my journey to become a software developer. Problem solving comes naturally to me, and that is the most important skill in software development!
